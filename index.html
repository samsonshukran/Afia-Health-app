General Health Information

Practice proper hand hygiene

These Include:

  • Washing hands with soap and water.
  • Using hand sanitizer when soap is unavailable.
  • Keeping nails trimmed and clean.

Control and prevent malaria through treated nets

Key Practices:

  • Use insecticide-treated bed nets.
  • Indoor spraying with insecticides.
  • Eliminate standing water to reduce mosquito breeding.

Vaccination protects against deadly diseases

Key Practices:

There are so many vaccines available to protect against various diseases.

  • BCG vaccine protects against tuberculosis.
  • Measles vaccine protects against measles.
  • Polio vaccine protects against poliomyelitis.
  • Hepatitis B vaccine protects against hepatitis B.
  • Ensure all vaccinations are up to date.
  • Educate communities about the benefits of vaccination.
  • Implement outreach programs to increase vaccination coverage.

Balanced diet ensures growth and recovery

Key Practices:

  • Include a variety of fruits and vegetables in the diet.
  • Ensure adequate protein intake from sources like beans, nuts, and meat.
  • Stay hydrated by drinking enough water throughout the day.

How do these foods ensure growth and recovery?

  • Fruits and vegetables provide essential vitamins and minerals.
  • Protein is crucial for building and repairing tissues.
  • Staying hydrated helps maintain overall bodily functions.

Public Health Overview

Definition

Public health is the science and practice of preventing disease, prolonging life, and promoting health through organized community efforts.

Key Pillars

  • Disease prevention
  • Health promotion
  • Health protection
  • Policy and planning
  • Research and evidence-based practice

Core Functions

  • Assessment: monitoring community health status
  • Policy development: creating strategies to address health needs
  • Assurance: ensuring health services are provided and accessible

Major Disciplines

  • Epidemiology
  • Biostatistics
  • Environmental health
  • Health policy and management
  • Social and behavioral sciences

Importance

Public health improves quality of life, increases life expectancy, reduces health disparities, and prepares communities for emerging health threats.

IDSR Reporting

IDSR Notes

Definition

IDSR is a strategy adopted by WHO/AFRO to strengthen public health surveillance and response systems in Africa.

Main Objectives

  • Detect and respond quickly to disease outbreaks.
  • Improve data collection, analysis, and reporting.
  • Strengthen laboratory support for public health surveillance.
  • Integrate multiple disease surveillance systems.
  • Promote evidence-based public health action.

Core Functions

  • Case detection and registration
  • Case confirmation
  • Reporting
  • Data analysis and interpretation
  • Preparedness and response
  • Feedback and communication
  • Monitoring and evaluation

Priority Diseases

Examples include:

  • Cholera
  • Measles
  • Meningitis
  • Polio
  • Yellow Fever
  • Ebola and other Viral Hemorrhagic Fevers

Immunization Notes

BCG Vaccine

Disease prevented: Tuberculosis

Age of administration: At birth

Injection site: Left upper arm (intradermal)

Certificate duration: Lifelong

DPT Vaccine

Disease prevented: Diphtheria, Pertussis, Tetanus

Age of administration: 6, 10, 14 weeks

Injection site: Thigh (intramuscular)

Certificate duration: 10 years (Tetanus booster needed)

Measles Vaccine

Disease prevented: Measles

Age of administration: 9 months and 18 months

Injection site: Right upper arm (subcutaneous)

Certificate duration: Lifelong

Yellow Fever Vaccine

Disease prevented: Yellow Fever

Age of administration: 9 months

Injection site: Left upper arm (subcutaneous)

Certificate duration: 10 years

Occupational Health Data

Overview

  • Occupational health focuses on physical, mental & social well-being of workers.
  • Aims: prevent work-related injuries/illnesses, promote healthy workplaces, fitness for work.
  • Stakeholders: employers, employees, OH professionals, regulators, unions.

Core Principles

  • Prevention — control hazards before harm occurs.
  • Risk assessment — evaluate hazards & consequences.
  • Hierarchy of controls — prioritize elimination > PPE.
  • Worker involvement — engage staff in safety.
  • Safe workplace — legal and ethical right.

Risk Assessment

  • Identify hazards: chemical, physical, biological, ergonomic, psychosocial, safety.
  • Decide who might be harmed & how.
  • Evaluate existing controls.
  • Record findings & act.
  • Review regularly & after changes.

Hierarchy of Controls

  1. Elimination
  2. Substitution
  3. Engineering controls
  4. Administrative controls
  5. PPE
  6. Training & supervision
  7. Health surveillance
  8. Emergency preparedness

Workplace Hazards

  • Physical: noise, vibration, heat/cold, radiation
  • Chemical: solvents, acids, asbestos, silica
  • Biological: blood-borne pathogens
  • Ergonomic: manual handling, repetitive strain
  • Psychosocial & Safety: stress, burnout, slips, violence

Occupational Diseases

  • Occupational asthma
  • COPD (dust/smoke)
  • Noise-induced hearing loss
  • Dermatitis
  • Musculoskeletal disorders
  • Cancers (e.g., mesothelioma)
  • Mental health issues (stress, burnout)
  • Cardiovascular diseases (hypertension, heart disease)
  • Respiratory diseases (asthma, COPD)

PPE

  • Must match hazard/task.
  • Fit-tested if required (respirators).
  • Cleaned, maintained, replaced.
  • Not the only control measure.
  • Training on correct use.
  • Regularly inspected & replaced as needed.
  • Inspection records maintained.

Health Surveillance

  • Pre-placement & fitness-for-work exams.
  • Periodic checks (audiometry, spirometry).
  • Incident/illness reporting & investigation.
  • Return-to-work programs.

Legal & Ethical

  • National OSH laws/regulations.
  • Employer duty of care, worker rights.
  • Record keeping (incidents, exposures).
  • Confidentiality & informed consent.

Emergency Response

  • Fire safety & evacuation plans.
  • Spill/chemical exposure response.
  • First aid & trauma readiness.
  • Incident command & communication.

Health Promotion in work places

  • Wellness: smoking cessation, mental health, fitness.
  • Nutrition & healthy lifestyle education.
  • Physical activity promotion.
  • Chronic disease management.
  • Vaccination programs (flu, hepatitis B).
  • Substance abuse prevention.
  • Ergonomics & posture education.
  • Stress reduction programs.
  • Work-life balance initiatives.
  • Employee assistance programs (EAPs).
  • Injury prevention & safety training.
  • Stress management & work-life balance.
  • Safe work procedures & injury prevention.
